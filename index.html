Dark Diary

I contributed a feature to Cataclysm: Dark Days Ahead (CDDA), an open-source survival roguelike largely developed by its community.

The Dark Diary feature I contributed is an in-game diary that allows the player to write down the events of the day and record the player's progress. It shows the character's progress made between entries and is designed to encourage the player to engage in creative writing.

Rebound Arena

In a sci-fi-setting, you compete in a sporting event and try to set a new record. Your task is to score as many points as possible within 10 minutes. To achieve this, you'll have to hit the balls that are coming at you against the wall as many times as possible, so that they bounce off. Meanwhile, you collect rings to get power-ups and dodge obstacles.

This game is a VR exergame. By playing the game, players are encouraged to engage in 10 minutes of moderate aerobic physical activity. The heart rate is measured and included in the game as a variable. There are two alternative game modes. The automatic version, in which the difficulty of the game is adjusted depending on the heart rate, and the feedback variant. In the latter, the players can see their heart rate in relation to the target heartrate and have the possibility to adjust the level of difficulty themselves. This game was developed as part of my master's thesis to find out how these two variants affect the player's experience. I developed the game within 12 weeks all by myself.

My contribution:

  • Game design
  • Implementation (Unity, C#)
  • UX
  • Sound
  • automatic adaptation

Anyway

In this 2D jump and run game, you are Manny and try to deliver delicious berries to your friend every day. But since every day there are new rules in the game, you have to re-evaluate how to achieve your goal continuously. The world doesn't change, but, due to the rules, the ways and possibilities available to you do so every day. Complete all the challenges, explore the world and discover all the secrets it holds for you.

This game was developed in a group/team of five as part of a practical exercise. Our goal was to design an interesting level which had to be mastered in various ways. Players are encouraged to re-evaluate the level and their respective possibilities to be able to choose a suitable way in the light of constantly changing rules. These for example can restrict the player’s abilities, forbid certain types of berries or pose additional challenges.

My contribution:

  • Game design
  • Implementation (Unity, C#)
  • Working with Git and Scrum

Eye Gaze Ghost

Your potion’s gone wrong. Now it's up to you to capture the escaped ghosts to prevent worse. But make sure you only catch the right ghosts. Look at the ghost you want to catch or aim at it with your magic wand.

This VR game was developed as part of a practical project in a group of three throughout one semester. The task was to develop a game for eye control of the HTC Vive Eye Pro which allows to compare the two interaction modalities “eye control” and “controller control” to each other. Therefore, two distinct game variants were developed. In the eye control condition, the ghost you want to catch has to be looked at, meanwhile in the controller condition, you have to point at it. In both conditions, the ghost is caught by pressing a button.

My contribution:

  • Game design
  • Implementation (Unity, C#)
